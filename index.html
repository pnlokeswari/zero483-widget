📋 FDA Form 483 Frequently Asked Questions

What is an FDA Form 483?

An FDA Form 483 is issued to firm management at the conclusion of an inspection when an investigator has observed any conditions that in their judgment may constitute violations of the Food Drug and Cosmetic (FD&C) Act and related Acts.

What is the purpose of a Form 483?

The FDA Form 483 notifies the company's management of objectionable conditions. It is presented and discussed with senior management at the conclusion of an inspection. Companies are encouraged to respond in writing with their corrective action plan and then implement that corrective action plan expeditiously.

Is a Form 483 a final Agency determination?

No. The FDA Form 483 does not constitute a final Agency determination of whether any condition is in violation of the FD&C Act or any of its relevant regulations. The FDA considers the Form 483 along with the Establishment Inspection Report (EIR), all evidence or documentation collected, and any responses made by the company before determining what further action, if any, is appropriate.

Is it an all-inclusive list of violations?

No. It is not an all-inclusive list. There may be other objectionable conditions that exist at the firm that are not cited on the FDA Form 483. Companies are responsible to take corrective action to address the cited observations as well as any related non-cited objectionable conditions.

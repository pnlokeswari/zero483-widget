EMA Recommends Revocation of Amgen's Tavneos Approval for Vasculitis

Published: 2026-06-26  |  Severity: Low  |  Source: Original Publication

Article Analysis & Summary

The European Medicines Agency's (EMA) Committee for Medicinal Products for Human Use (CHMP) has delivered a significant recommendation: the revocation of marketing authorization for Amgen's drug, Tavneos (avacopan). This decision, if finalized, would mean that Tavneos can no longer be sold or used in the European Union. Tavneos is a critical treatment for ANCA-associated vasculitis (AAV), a rare and serious autoimmune disease that causes inflammation of blood vessels, potentially leading to organ damage. While the specific reasons for this recommendation were not detailed in the brief update, such actions typically stem from a re-evaluation of the drug's benefit-risk profile or new safety concerns arising from post-market surveillance.

Context & Background

Tavneos, an oral C5aR inhibitor, represented a significant advancement in treating ANCA-associated vasculitis, offering a steroid-sparing option that could reduce reliance on corticosteroids and their associated side effects. Originally developed by ChemoCentryx, the drug gained further prominence following Amgen's acquisition of the company in 2022. Regulatory actions to revoke approval are rare and underscore the stringent oversight maintained by health authorities to ensure medicines remain safe and effective throughout their lifecycle.

Key Implications & Public Impact

  • Patient Treatment Shifts: Patients currently on Tavneos in Europe may need to transition to alternative therapies, guided by their healthcare providers.

FDA Recall Alert: Elevate Oral Care's Povi-One Oral Antiseptic Found Subpotent – What You Need to Know

Published: 2026-06-04  |  Severity: High  |  Source: Original Publication

Article Analysis & Summary

This is a **Featured Article** on Zero483, shining a light on critical regulatory updates impacting public health and the pharmaceutical industry. The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has announced a significant Class II recall for Povi-One, a 10% Povidone-Iodine Oral Antiseptic, packaged by Elevate Oral Care, LLC. This nationwide recall, currently ongoing, affects a substantial quantity of the product – specifically, 8,132 6-packs and 2,262 2-packs – primarily due to a critical manufacturing issue: sub potency.

Sub potency means that the active ingredient, povidone-iodine, is present at a concentration lower than what is stated on the product label. For an antiseptic like Povi-One, which is designed to reduce germs in the mouth, this deficiency directly compromises its effectiveness. Patients and consumers using a subpotent antiseptic may not receive the intended protective benefits, potentially leading to an increased risk of infection or inadequate microbial control, especially in situations where maintaining oral hygiene is paramount.

The FDA's classification of this as a Class II recall indicates that exposure to the product may cause temporary or medically reversible adverse health consequences, or that the probability of serious adverse health consequences is remote. While not a Class I recall (which indicates serious health risks or death), a Class II recall still highlights a significant concern for public safety, as the product fails to perform its essential function effectively. This classification underscores the FDA's commitment to ensuring that medical products meet their stated specifications and are safe and effective for their intended use.

Elevate Oral Care, LLC, based in West Palm Beach, FL, is the firm responsible for packaging this product, with NDC 57511-0611-1. The recall initiated by the company reflects its responsibility to address quality issues once identified. This incident serves as a crucial reminder for both consumers to check their medications and for healthcare professionals to stay informed about product recalls that could impact patient care.

Context & Background

Povidone-iodine is a well-established and widely used broad-spectrum antiseptic, recognized for its efficacy against bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protozoa. It works by releasing iodine, which then denatures proteins and nucleic acids in microbial cells, effectively killing or inhibiting their growth. In the context of oral care, a 10% povidone-iodine solution, often diluted or applied directly, is commonly used as a pre-operative rinse before dental procedures, to manage gingivitis, or as a general oral antiseptic to reduce oral microbial load, thereby minimizing the risk of infection.

The effectiveness of any antiseptic hinges entirely on its concentration of the active ingredient. A subpotent antiseptic is, by definition, less effective or even ineffective at killing or inhibiting the growth of microorganisms. For oral care, this could mean that patients using Povi-One before a dental procedure might not be adequately protected against infection, or individuals using it for daily oral hygiene might not achieve the desired level of microbial control. This not only undermines the product's therapeutic promise but also erodes consumer trust in essential health products.

This recall highlights the critical importance of stringent quality control and Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) within the pharmaceutical and medical device industries. Even for products that are packaged by a third party, the responsibility for ensuring product quality remains paramount across the supply chain. Manufacturers and packagers are held accountable for ensuring that every batch released to the market meets precise specifications, from the active ingredient's strength to its stability and purity. Failures in these areas can have far-reaching consequences, affecting patient outcomes, healthcare provider practices, and the reputation of companies involved.

Key Implications & Public Impact

  • Compromised Patient Safety and Efficacy: Patients and consumers relying on Povi-One for its antiseptic properties may not receive the intended protection, increasing their risk of oral infections, especially those undergoing dental procedures or with compromised immune systems.
  • Erosion of Consumer and Healthcare Provider Trust: Incidents of sub potency can damage public confidence in over-the-counter and prescription products, leading to skepticism about product labels and overall quality assurance within the industry.
  • Significant Financial and Reputational Damage for Elevate Oral Care: The recall incurs substantial costs related to retrieving products, investigating the root cause, potential fines, and lost sales. More importantly, it can severely impact the company's brand image and future market standing.
  • Disruption to Supply Chain and Retailers: Retailers, pharmacies, and healthcare facilities that stocked Povi-One must remove and return the recalled products, causing operational disruptions and potential revenue loss.
  • Increased Scrutiny from Regulatory Bodies: The FDA will likely increase its oversight and potentially conduct further inspections of Elevate Oral Care's facilities and quality systems to ensure robust compliance and prevent future similar incidents.
Key Takeaways & Recommended Actions:
  • For Consumers and Patients: Immediately check your medicine cabinets for Povi-One, 10% Povidone-Iodine Oral Antiseptic (NDC 57511-0611-1). If you possess the recalled product, discontinue use immediately and follow the instructions provided by Elevate Oral Care for its return or disposal. Consult your healthcare provider if you have any health concerns or believe you may have been adversely affected.
  • For Healthcare Professionals and Dentists: Review your inventory for Povi-One. Remove and quarantine any affected products to prevent further distribution and use. Inform patients who may have used this product about the recall and advise them on appropriate alternative antiseptic solutions or follow-up care.
  • For Retailers and Distributors: Identify and immediately remove all recalled lots of Povi-One from your shelves and inventory. Contact Elevate Oral Care for instructions on returning the affected products and for guidance on managing this recall efficiently within your supply chain.
  • For Pharmaceutical Manufacturers and Packagers: This recall serves as a critical reminder to reinforce rigorous quality control measures, particularly around active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) potency testing and manufacturing consistency. Ensure all stages of production and packaging adhere to the highest quality standards to prevent such incidents.
